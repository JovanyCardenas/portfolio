About Me

Hello! My name is Jovany Cardenas Vargas, and I am years old. I was born and raised in Santa Maria, California, and come from a family with two younger brothers and two older half-brothers. My parents, originally from Mexico, work in the strawberry fields, and their strong work ethic has inspired my own dedication.

I discovered my passion for Computer Science during a high school web design class, which led me to pursue an Associate of Science in Computer Science at Allan Hancock College. My coursework includes Intro to Programming (Java), Computer Organization (Assembly), and Discrete Structures. I plan to transfer to Cal Poly San Luis Obispo to complete my bachelor’s degree and explore areas like AI, cybersecurity, and software development.

Beyond academics, I am actively involved in SkillsUSA, where I’ve earned 3 state gold medals, a national silver medal, and a national bronze medal. I’ve held leadership roles as Chapter Secretary, Vice-President, and currently Chapter President.

Professionally, I work at the Allan Hancock College Career Center, supporting students with job searches, resume writing, and career counseling prep. I also served as a Interim Program Specialist for the College Corps program, managing events, partnerships, and fellow support.
