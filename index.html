About Me

Hello! My name is Jovany Cardenas Vargas, and I am 21 years old. I was born and raised in Santa Maria, California, and come from a family with two younger brothers and two older half-brothers. My parents, originally from Mexico, work in the strawberry fields, and their strong work ethic has inspired my own dedication.

I discovered my passion for Computer Science during a high school web design class, which led me to pursue an Associate of Science in Computer Science at Allan Hancock College. My coursework includes Intro to Programming (Java), Computer Organization (Assembly), and Discrete Structures. I plan to transfer to Cal Poly San Luis Obispo to complete my bachelor’s degree and explore areas like AI, cybersecurity, and software development.

Beyond academics, I am actively involved in SkillsUSA, where I’ve earned three California state gold medals, a national silver medal, and a national bronze medal in Computer Programming. I’ve also served in chapter leadership as Secretary, Vice President, and President.

Professionally, I currently work as a Cybersecurity Intern with the Allan Hancock College IT Department, where I help support cybersecurity awareness, data center operations, website quality assurance, and security-related projects. I also continue serving as a Student Worker for the Career Center and College Corps, supporting students, events, outreach, and program operations.
